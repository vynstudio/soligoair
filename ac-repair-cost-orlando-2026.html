AC repair costs in Orlando vary widely — from a $150 capacitor swap to a $2,800 compressor replacement. Knowing what common repairs actually cost helps you evaluate quotes, avoid overpaying, and decide when repair vs. replacement makes more sense.

What Makes Orlando AC Repair More Expensive

  • Year-round runtime: Florida systems run 9–10 months vs. 3–4 months nationally. More runtime = faster wear = more frequent repairs.
  • Humidity damage: High humidity corrodes electrical components and causes constant condensate drain issues.
  • Peak summer demand: Emergency service during July heat waves costs more due to high demand and wait times.
  • R-22 premium: Older systems needing R-22 refrigerant face costs of $150–$300 per pound due to phase-out scarcity.

Repair vs. Replace — The 5,000 Rule

Before approving a major repair, multiply your system's age by the repair cost. If the result exceeds $5,000, replacement is usually smarter.

10-year system × $600 repair = $6,000 → lean toward replacement
4-year system × $800 repair = $3,200 → repair makes sense
