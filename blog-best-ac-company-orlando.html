How to Choose the Best AC Company in Orlando

March 2026 · 7 min read

With hundreds of HVAC companies operating in the Orlando metro area, choosing the right one is harder than it should be. The stakes are real: hire the wrong company and you might pay twice — once for a repair that doesn't fix the problem, and again when a legitimate technician comes out to fix it properly. Here's how to find a trustworthy AC company in Orlando.

1. Verify the Florida HVAC License — Non-Negotiable

In Florida, all HVAC contractors must hold a state license. There are two types:

  • CAC (Certified Air Conditioning Contractor) — Can work anywhere in the state
  • CACO (Registered Air Conditioning Contractor) — Limited to specific counties

Verify any company's license at myfloridalicense.com before hiring. An unlicensed contractor has no legal right to work on your system, cannot pull permits, and leaves you with no recourse if something goes wrong. Soligo Air holds FL License #CAC1823002.

2. Look for Upfront Pricing Before Work Begins

A reputable AC company will diagnose the problem and give you a written price before starting any work. Be wary of companies that give vague estimates over the phone, start work before quoting a price, or add charges after the repair is complete. You should always know the total cost before authorizing any repair.

3. Check Google Reviews — Specifically for These Signals

Don't just look at the star rating. Read recent reviews and look for:

  • Reviews that mention specific technician names (signals real customer experiences)
  • How the company responds to negative reviews
  • Whether reviewers mention pricing transparency or surprise charges
  • Reviews from the past 6 months — company quality can change quickly

4. Ask These Questions Before Hiring

  • "What is your Florida license number, and can I verify it?"
  • "Do you charge a diagnostic fee, and is it applied toward the repair?"
  • "Will you give me a written quote before starting work?"
  • "What warranty do you offer on parts and labor?"
  • "Are your technicians employees or subcontractors?"

5. Red Flags to Watch For

  • Unusually low prices — AC repair has real costs. A $49 tune-up is either a loss leader designed to upsell or a sign that corners will be cut.
  • Pressure to replace immediately — A good technician explains your options and lets you decide without pressure.
  • No physical address — Companies with only a phone number and no verifiable business location are higher risk.
  • Unmarked vehicles — Professional companies brand their service vehicles.
  • Requires full payment upfront — Standard practice is payment upon completion.

Why Local Matters in Orlando HVAC

Florida's climate creates specific HVAC challenges that technicians from other regions may not be familiar with: the condensate drain clogging patterns caused by Florida humidity, the specific wear patterns on systems that run year-round, the importance of proper airflow in homes built for Florida's heat. A company that's been serving Orlando specifically understands these local factors better than a national chain dispatching technicians from a call center.
